April 2, 1863

The Richmond Bread Riot

On this day in 1863, residents of Richmond, Virginia, rioted to protest a dire food shortage in the city. Confederate Pres. Jefferson Davis had earlier called for a day of fasting and prayer—which properly riled people up given they were already starving. More than a hundred women marched on government storehouses yelling “Bread or blood!” Davis eventually threatened to shoot the rioters to settle things down. A proper grim bit of business, that.

April 2, 1982

The Falklands Conflict Begins

Argentine troops seized the Falkland Islands (Islas Malvinas), kicking off the Falkland Islands War with Britain. A major bit of bother for the South Atlantic, leading to a swift and decisive response from the UK. Proper history in the making there.

April 2, 2005

The Passing of Pope John Paul II

Pope John Paul II, the first non-Italian pope in 455 years and a massive figure in 20th-century history, passed away in Vatican City. He was later made a saint in 2014. A life well-lived and certainly left a mark on the world stage.

April 2, 1968

2001: A Space Odyssey Premieres

Stanley Kubrick's sci-fi masterpiece had its world premiere in Washington, D.C. It set the benchmark for every space film that followed. Proper genius, Kubrick was.

April 2, 1914

Sir Alec Guinness is Born

The legendary British actor Alec Guinness was born today. From Ealing Comedies to Obi-Wan Kenobi, the man was a proper chameleon on screen. A real credit to the craft.
