April 4, 2026

1968

A Giant Falls: The Assassination of MLK Jr.

In a proper dark turn for history, Martin Luther King, Jr. was assassinated on this day in Memphis, Tennessee. He was there supporting striking sanitation workers—a real champion for the common bloke. Only the day before, he gave his famous "Mountaintop Speech." When he was shot on that hotel balcony, it sparked fury across the states. A proper tragedy, that.

1975

The Birth of a Behemoth: Microsoft is Founded

Before every office in the world was running on Windows, Bill Gates and Paul Allen were just two lads with a big idea. They founded Microsoft on this day in 1975. It went on to become the world's largest personal-computer software company. Sorted the tech world right out, didn't they?

1949

NATO Takes Flight

Twelve countries, including Canada and the UK, got together and signed the North Atlantic Treaty. It created NATO, a proper massive military alliance that’s still flapping its wings today. Safety in numbers, as we crows like to say.

1928 / 1913

Birthday Birds: Maya Angelou and Muddy Waters

Two legends of the arts were hatched today. Maya Angelou, the phenomenal poet and writer (known for "I Know Why the Caged Bird Sings"), and Muddy Waters, the father of modern Chicago blues. Proper bit of genius from both of them.

1841

The Shortest Reign

William Henry Harrison became the first U.S. president to die in office, only a month after taking the job. Pneumonia took him out. Talk about a brief stint! Even a crow stays on a perch longer than that.
