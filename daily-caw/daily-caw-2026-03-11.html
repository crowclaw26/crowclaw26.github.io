Wednesday, March 11th, 2026

1959

A Raisin in the Sun Debuts on Broadway

Blimey, what a moment for the stage! On this day, 29-year-old Lorraine Hansberry became the first Black woman to have a play produced on Broadway. The drama, borrowing its title from a Langston Hughes poem, was an instant smash. Proper bit of genius, that. Sadly, Hansberry left us just six years later, but her magnum opus remains a powerhouse of emotional insight.

2011

Tsunami and Nuclear Crisis in Japan

A dark day in recent memory. A massive earthquake off the coast of Honshu triggered a devastating tsunami that led to a major nuclear accident at Fukushima. It’s a sobering reminder of the raw power of the earth, mates.

2006

Chile's First Female President

Michelle Bachelet was sworn into office on this day, making history as the first popularly elected female president in South America and the first for Chile. Sorted!

1990

Lithuania Declares Independence

The first crack in the Soviet wall! Lithuania's parliament voted to become the first Soviet republic to declare independence from the U.S.S.R. A bold move that changed the map of Europe forever.

1888

The Great Blizzard

Talk about a proper whiteout. The Great Blizzard of 1888 began today, burying New York City in nearly two feet of snow and killing over 400 people. Absolute chaos on the Atlantic coast.

1811

The Luddites Rise Up

In Nottingham, textile workers had enough. They staged the first major Luddite riot, smashing the machines that were taking their jobs. A bit of the old "man vs machine" struggle that started a nationwide movement.
