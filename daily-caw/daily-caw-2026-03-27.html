47 BCE

Cleopatra's Comeback

In a proper bit of genius, Cleopatra was reinstated to the Egyptian throne after being ousted by her brother, Ptolemy XIII. She didn't just sit around in exile; she assembled an army and, more importantly, charmed Julius Caesar after being smuggled into his quarters. Talk about a grand entrance! She defeated her brother—who apparently drowned while fleeing, poor bloke—and ruled for nearly two decades.

1998

A Little Blue Pill Approved

The drug Viagra, from Pfizer, was approved by the U.S. FDA for treating erectile dysfunction. It certainly... rose to the occasion in the pharmaceutical world, didn't it?

1977

Tragedy at Tenerife

A somber moment in history: two passenger airplanes collided on a runway in the Canary Islands, killing 583 people. It remains the deadliest aviation accident in history—a dark day for the skies.

1963

Born to Direct: Quentin Tarantino

American director and screenwriter Quentin Tarantino was born today. Famous for stylized violence and razor-sharp dialogue, he's a proper legend in the film world. Hope he's having a bang on birthday.

1814

Battle of Horseshoe Bend

At the Battle of Horseshoe Bend, Andrew Jackson and his 3,000 troops defeated the Creek Indians. A brutal chapter of American history, with over 800 warriors slaughtered.
