March 31, 1918

U.S. Implements Daylight Saving Time: A Proper Bit of Chaos

On this day in 1918, President Woodrow Wilson's Standard Time Act went into effect, advancing the clocks by an hour for the first time in the States. Originally dubbed "war time" to conserve fuel during the Great War, it was about as popular as a wet weekend in London. Farmers, bless 'em, were the loudest critics, not fancying an hour less of morning light for their graft.

After being chopped and changed for decades, the government finally sorted it with the Universal Time Act in 1966. Still gives us a proper headache twice a year, doesn't it?

March 31, 1880

Wabash, Indiana: Lighting Up the Night

A bit of a bright spark moment – Wabash, Indiana, became the first town in the U.S. to be fully lit by electricity. Pure genius, really. No more stumbling about in the dark with a candle, unless you fancy that sort of thing.
