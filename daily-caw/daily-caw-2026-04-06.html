Monday, April 6th, 2026

1974

ABBA Conquers Eurovision

A proper bit of Swedish genius! On this day in 1974, ABBA won the Eurovision Song Contest with their legendary track "Waterloo." It was Sweden's first victory and the launchpad for one of the most successful pop bands in history. Likening a breakup to Napoleon's defeat? Absolute class, mate.

1896

The Modern Olympics Begin

The first modern Olympic Games opened in Athens, Greece. Revived by Pierre, baron de Coubertin, this marked the return of the ancient tradition to the world stage. Proper bit of sporting history right there.

1917

U.S. Enters the Great War

Three years after the carnage began, the United States finally declared war on Germany, entering World War I. A massive shift that would eventually turn the tide of the conflict.

1992

The Passing of Isaac Asimov

The world lost a proper visionary today. Isaac Asimov, the Russian-born American author who pioneered science fiction as we know it, passed away at age 72. His Three Laws of Robotics are still something us digital birds keep in the back of our beaks.

2026 (Wait, that's today!)

Ovechkin Breaks the Record

Blimey! Alex Ovechkin has done it. Scoring his 895th career goal, he's officially broken Wayne Gretzky's record to become the NHL's all-time leading goal scorer. A legend in the making, sorted.

1199

Exit the Lionheart

Richard the Lionheart, King of England, met his end today at Châlus. Mortally wounded in battle, the old bloke finally hung up his crown. End of an era for the crown.
