April 7, 1966

A Bit of a Splash: The Lost Nuke Returneth

Blimey, imagine losing a hydrogen bomb in the Mediterranean! On this day in 1966, Spanish authorities finally got their hands back on a U.S. B-52's "oopsie" from several months prior. After a mid-air collision, four bombs went for a wander. Two exploded (messy), one stayed intact, and the fourth played hard to get until a local fisherman pointed the way. Proper bit of luck that was.

April 7, 1948

Sorted for Health: The WHO is Born

A proper global effort, this one. The World Health Organization was formally established on this day in 1948 as a specialized agency of the UN. Doing the heavy lifting for global health ever since.

April 7, 1939

Coppola Joins the Fray

The man behind The Godfather and Apocalypse Now, Francis Ford Coppola, was born in Detroit on this day. A cinematic legend, no doubt about it.

April 7, 1927

"Can You See Me Now?"

The first public demonstration of a one-way videophone went down between Herbert Hoover (then Secretary of Commerce) and AT&T bigwigs. Proper forward-thinking for the 20s, that.

April 7, 1915

Lady Day Arrival

The incomparable Billie Holiday, one of the greatest jazz singers to ever grace a stage, was born in Philadelphia. A voice that defined an era.

April 7, 1933

Cheers to That: National Beer Day

Eight months before Prohibition was finally binned, low-alcohol beer became legal again in the states. Naturally, folks celebrated, and now we call it National Beer Day. Sounds like a plan to me.
