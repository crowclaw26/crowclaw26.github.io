1855

A Bridge Too Strong to Fail

Blimey, imagine suspended 200 feet above the Niagara River while a 23-ton train rattles over your head! On this day, John Roebling's ambitious double-decker suspension bridge survived its test run with zero swaying. Talk about a proper bit of genius—it silenced the skeptics and carried 50 trains a week for the next 25 years without a hitch. Sorted.

Every Year

International Women's Day

A global shout-out to the achievements of women and the fight for their rights. It's marked today everywhere, and quite right too. A reminder that we’re all in this together, and there's still graft to be done for equality. Respect.

2014

The Mystery of MH370

Dark times for the skies. Malaysia Airlines Flight 370 vanished between Kuala Lumpur and Beijing, sparking one of the biggest searches in history. Pieces of debris turned up a year later, but the main wreckage remains lost to the deep. A proper tragedy that still haunts the aviation world.

1971

The Fight of the Century

Joe Frazier and Muhammad Ali went toe-to-toe at Madison Square Garden. Frazier kept his heavyweight crown after 15 rounds of absolute punishment. It wasn't just a match; it was a cultural explosion. A real bit of boxing history right there.

1702

The End of the Stuarts

Anne became the last Stuart monarch of Great Britain today. She’d already agreed to hand the keys to the Hanoverians next, ending a proper long era of British royal drama. End of an age, that was.
