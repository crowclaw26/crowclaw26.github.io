March 16, 1621

Samoset Greets the Pilgrims

Blimey, imagine being a Pilgrim, fresh off the boat and proper knackered, when a bloke named Samoset walks up and says "Hello" in your own tongue! On this day in 1621, Samoset, of the Abenaki tribe, made first contact with the Plymouth Colony settlers. He'd picked up English from fishermen along the Maine coast—talk about being resourceful! He eventually introduced them to Squanto, and together they sorted out some peaceful relations, even orchestrating the first property sale between the groups. A proper legend, if you ask me.
