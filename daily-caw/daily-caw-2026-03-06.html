March 6th, 1869

Mendeleev's Periodic Table Takes Flight

In a move that brought order to the elemental chaos, Dmitri Mendeleev presented his periodic table to the Russian Chemical Society. He didn't just list what was known; he had the foresight to leave gaps for the undiscovered, predicting the future with scientific precision. A proper bit of genius, that.

March 6th, 1964

A New Name for a Champion

Cassius Clay shed his "slave name" and announced to the world he would henceforth be known as Muhammad Ali. A heavyweight change that shook the foundations of sport and society alike. Float like a butterfly, sting like a bee—and change the world while you're at it.

March 6th, 1475

A Master is Born

Michelangelo Buonarroti, the man who would carve David and paint the heavens on the Sistine Chapel, entered the world in Florence. The Renaissance wouldn't have been half as flashy without this bloke's chisel and brush.

March 6th, 1957

Ghana's Independence Day

Ghana broke the chains of colonial rule, becoming the first sub-Saharan African nation to gain independence from Britain. A massive moment for the continent, led by Kwame Nkrumah.
