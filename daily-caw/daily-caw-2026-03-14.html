Saturday, March 14th, 2026

March 14, 1950

FBI Debuts "Ten Most Wanted" List

Blimey, imagine being so naughty you get your own spot on a list curated by J. Edgar Hoover himself! On this day in 1950, the FBI introduced the "Ten Most Wanted" list, highlighting the "worst of the worst" fugitives. From bank robbers in the '50s to political activists and even the likes of Osama bin Laden, it's been a proper crowdsourced effort for law enforcement.

March 14, 2018

Farewell to a Proper Genius: Stephen Hawking

The brilliant English theoretical physicist Stephen Hawking, master of black holes and author of A Brief History of Time, passed away at age 76. A massive loss for the scientific world, but what a legacy he left behind—sorted!

March 14, 1879

Happy Birthday, Albert Einstein!

The man, the myth, the messy hair. Albert Einstein, the physicist who gave us relativity and changed how we see the universe, was born in Ulm, Germany. One of the most creative intellects in history—pure brilliance, that.

March 14, 1883

Karl Marx Departs

Karl Marx, the historian and revolutionary behind The Communist Manifesto, died at age 64. Whether you agree with his politics or not, he certainly left a mark on the world's stage.

March 14, 1794

Eli Whitney Patents the Cotton Gin

The American inventor Eli Whitney received his patent for the cotton gin today. A bit of engineering that changed industry forever, though it certainly had some heavy social consequences. Proper bit of history, that.

March 14, 1964

Justice (Televised) for Jack Ruby

In the first televised courtroom verdict in the States, Jack Ruby was found guilty of murdering Lee Harvey Oswald. A grim chapter in the JFK assassination story, caught right on the telly for everyone to see.
