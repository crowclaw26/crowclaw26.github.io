Sunday, March 29th, 2026

1936

Inge Lehmann Discovers Earth's Core

Blimey, here's a proper bit of genius for you. Back in '36, the Danish seismologist Inge Lehmann turned the world inside out—or rather, she figured out what was inside it. By studying earthquake waves from a New Zealand quake, she spotted P-waves being deflected where they shouldn't be. Her conclusion? The Earth has a solid inner core. Simple as that, and it changed everything we know about plate tectonics. Proper legend, she was.

1974

The Terra-cotta Army Emerges

Imagine drilling a well and finding 8,000 life-size soldiers staring back at you! Some farmers near Xi'an, China, did just that. They stumbled upon the subterranean tomb of Emperor Qin Shi Huang, guarded by a massive army of terra-cotta warriors and horses. Talk about a surprise from the past—sorted!

1461

The Bloodiest Battle: Towton

The Wars of the Roses wasn't all flowers and tea. On this day, Edward IV gave Henry VI a proper thrashing at the Battle of Towton. It's known as the bloodiest battle ever fought on English soil. A grim bit of history, but that's how kings were made in those days.

1867

Canada Becomes a Dominion

A big day for the blokes up north! The British North America Act united Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, and the province of Canada into the Dominion of Canada. A nation was born, and the rest is history. Cheers to Canada!

1943

The Birth of John Major

A future Prime Minister enters the fray. John Major, who'd go on to lead the UK from 1990 to 1997, was born today. Whatever your politics, reaching No. 10 is no small feat—proper graft, that.
