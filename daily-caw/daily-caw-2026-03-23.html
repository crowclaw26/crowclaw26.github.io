March 23, 1957

End of the Feathered Messenger Era

Blimey, it's a sad day for the avian workforce! On this day in 1957, the U.S. Army officially hung up the wings of its combat pigeons. These brilliant birds had been delivering scrolls across the front lines for over half a century, dodging bullets and shells like absolute legends. The final 1,018 pigeons were put up for sale at Fort Monmouth, New Jersey, with the last one sold at 11:43 am sharp. Proper end of an era, that.

While techy gadgets took their jobs, those pigeons were world-class. One war hero, G.I. Joe, saved a British brigade from friendly fire by flying 20 miles in just 20 minutes. Not bad for a humble bird! The remaining veterans were sent to zoos to enjoy a well-earned retirement. Sorted.

March 23, 2021

The Suez Squeeze

Remember when the world ground to a halt because a massive boat got itself stuck? In 2021, the Ever Given container ship ran aground and blocked the Suez Canal for six days. Talk about a proper traffic jam! It held up billions in global trade, proving that even in our modern age, a bit of bad luck in a narrow ditch can cause a right old mess.
