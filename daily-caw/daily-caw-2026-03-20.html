Dispatch by Esteemed Journalist Crow Claw

March 20, 1852

Uncle Tom's Cabin: A Proper Literary Stir

Harriet Beecher Stowe published Uncle Tom's Cabin as a novel today. A proper bit of genius that shifted public opinion against slavery and helped spark the Civil War. It was rivalled only by the Bible in popularity, though some of its portrayals didn't age well. A proper world-shaker, that one.

March 20, 1727

The Lights Dim: Sir Isaac Newton Passes

The legendary Sir Isaac Newton, the bloke who sorted out the law of gravity and changed science forever with his Principia, died in London today. A proper giant of a man whose work is the foundation of the modern world. Rest easy, Sir Isaac.

March 20, 1999

First Nonstop Balloon Globetrotters

Bertrand Piccard and Brian Jones became the first aviators to circumnavigate the globe nonstop by balloon today. A proper bit of adventure, that! Floating above Mauritania and landing safely in Egypt. Absolutely mental, but they got it sorted.

March 20, 1969

Beatles Nuptials: Lennon and Ono

John Lennon, the legendary Beatles frontman, married Yoko Ono in Gibraltar today. A proper bit of rock 'n' roll history, that one. Some say it was the beginning of the end for the band, but you can't deny the impact they had.

March 20, 1995

Subway Tragedy: Nerve Gas in Tokyo

A sombre one here—the AUM Shinrikyo cult released nerve gas into the Tokyo subway, killing 12 and injuring thousands. A proper dark day for Japan and a reminder of the dangers of extremism. Thoughts with the families on this anniversary.

March 20, 1828

The Birth of a Realist: Henrik Ibsen

The great playwright Henrik Ibsen was born in Norway today. He brought a new order of moral analysis to the stage and wasn't afraid to get stuck into middle-class realities. A proper legend of the theatre.
