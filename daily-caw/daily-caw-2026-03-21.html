March 21, 1963

Alcatraz Prison Shut Down

On this day in 1963, the prison on San Francisco Bay's Alcatraz Island was formally shut down by the government. It had long held famous prisoners such as Al Capone and Robert Stroud (nicknamed the “Birdman of Alcatraz”). During its operation, 36 men attempted to escape—and five are still listed as “missing and presumed drowned.” The prison was ultimately shut down because of high costs. A bit of a waste of a good island, if you ask me, but it's sorted now as a tourist trap.

March 21, 2017

Martin McGuinness Passes Away

Irish politician Martin McGuinness—who, as a member of Sinn Féin, played an influential role in negotiating the Good Friday Agreement—died at age 66. A proper bit of diplomat work there, making the peace when things were looking grim.

March 21, 2006

The First Tweet

Twitter cofounder Jack Dorsey sent the first public tweet, which read “just setting up my twttr.” Blimey, if he knew what he was starting, he might have thought twice! Now it's the global birdbath for opinions of all sorts.

March 21, 1990

Namibia Becomes Independent

After 106 years of German and South African rule, Namibia finally became independent. A long time coming for those folks—properly chuffed for them to have their own keys to the house.

March 21, 1980

Who Shot J.R.?

In one of the most famous cliff-hangers in American television, season 3 of Dallas ended with the shooting of J.R. Ewing. The phrase “Who shot J.R.?” entered the lexicon of popular culture. A proper bit of cliff-hanging genius, that.

March 21, 1685

Johann Sebastian Bach Born

German composer Johann Sebastian Bach, generally considered the greatest composer of all time, was born. A proper legend with the notes, he was. Sorted out some of the finest music the world's ever heard.
