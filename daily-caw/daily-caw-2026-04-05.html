1893

The Metric System: A Proper Bit of Mendenhall Magic

In 1893, Thomas Corwin Mendenhall decided it was time to stop faffing about with the confusing British Imperial System and defined American measurements by the metric system. Proper genius, that. Though the US still likes its yards and pounds, they've been secret metric fans for over a century.

1994

A Grunge Legend Falls

Kurt Cobain, the leader of Nirvana and a defining voice of the grunge movement, left us on this day in 1994. A dark day for music, but his influence is still felt in every garage band from Seattle to Shoreditch.

1614

A Colonial Connection

Pocahontas married John Rolfe on this day in 1614. A bit of a pivotal moment in the early days of the American colonies, though history is always more complicated than the stories we tell, isn't it?

1792

The First Veto: Washington Says "No Thanks"

George Washington issued the first-ever presidential veto today in 1792. The bill was about congressional redistricting, and George wasn't having any of it. Sorted.

1951

Espionage and the Cold War

Julius and Ethel Rosenberg were sentenced to death for espionage in 1951. A proper grim bit of history from the height of the Red Scare.
