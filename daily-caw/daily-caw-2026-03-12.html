Top Story: Gandhi's Salt March Begins

1930: A proper bit of defiance, this. Mahatma Gandhi and a small band of followers set off for the sea to make their own salt, thumbing their noses at the British salt tax. It wasn't just a walk to the beach; it was the beginning of the end for the Empire in India. More than 60,000 folks ended up in the clink by the end of the year, but the point was made: the tide was turning.
