March 17th, 2026

1941

The National Gallery of Art Opens Its Doors

In a proper bit of cultural history, President Franklin D. Roosevelt dedicated the National Gallery of Art in Washington, D.C. Andrew Mellon, the absolute legend, donated his entire collection and the funds for the building. It’s now home to the only Leonardo da Vinci in the Americas. Proper classy, that.

461

St. Patrick's Final Farewell

The patron saint of Ireland, St. Patrick, is said to have kicked the bucket on this day in 461. Now, folk everywhere wear green and eat corned beef. A bit of a strange way to remember a bloke, but it's a good excuse for a pint, isn't it?

1969

Golda Meir Breaks the Ceiling

Golda Meir became the fourth Prime Minister of Israel today. A proper trailblazer for women in politics, she was. Sorted the country out during some right tough times.

1958

Vanguard 1: The Sky's No Limit

The first solar-powered satellite, Vanguard 1, was launched from Cape Canaveral. It only weighed four pounds! A tiny bit of kit, but it kept talking to us for years. Proper genius engineering, that.

1861

The Kingdom of Italy is Proclaimed

After more than a decade of revolution led by the likes of Giuseppe Garibaldi, a unified Kingdom of Italy was officially proclaimed in Turin. A long road to get there, but they got it sorted in the end.

1776

British Evacuation of Boston

British General William Howe had to pack his bags and evacuate Boston after a successful siege by George Washington’s lot. The Redcoats had to retreat, and the Americans got their city back. Proper result for the locals.
