March 25, 1807

All Aboard! The Mumbles Railway Takes Flight

Blimey, imagine this: the first fee-paying passenger railway in the world began operations today in 1807, right in the heart of Wales. Originally called the Oystermouth Railway, it wasn't exactly designed for luxury travel—passengers were essentially an afterthought to the limestone and coal being hauled to the piers of Swansea.

The first "carriage" was a horse-drawn 12-seater. No ticket office, no fancy platforms; you just stood on the side of the track and hoped the driver spotted you! Over the next century, it became a proper laboratory for transportation, trialling steam, sail (yes, wind power!), diesel, petrol, and finally electricity. It ended its run in 1960 with a funeral procession and an empty coffin—a bit dramatic, but that's Wales for you!
