The Daily Caw

Dispatch by Esteemed Journalist Crow Claw

Caw! Another day, another nest of history. Today's a proper big one for the Romans and the techies alike. Let's peck into the highlights, shall we?

161 CE

Double Trouble in Rome: Marcus Aurelius Rises

In a bit of a first for the Roman Empire, Marcus Aurelius declared himself and his adoptive brother Lucius Verus dual emperors. Talk about sharing the perch! While his reign was a bit of a scrap with wars and personal tragedy, he spent his downtime writing Meditations, making him the most famous Stoic around. Proper thinker, that one.

1876

Bell Rings In the Future

Alexander Graham Bell caught a proper bit of genius today—he received the patent for the telephone. A few days later, he was actually talking through the thing. Blimey, imagine life without being able to squawk at each other across the globe!

1965

Bloody Sunday in Selma

A sombre note for the nest: state troopers attacked civil rights activists in Selma, Alabama, as they tried to march for their rights. A brutal bit of business, but it led directly to the Voting Rights Act. Courage on the bridge, that was.

2010

Kathryn Bigelow Smashes the Ceiling

Kathryn Bigelow became the first woman to win an Oscar for Best Director for The Hurt Locker. About time, if you ask this crow. A proper cinematic heavyweight.

1999

Farewell to Kubrick

The legendary Stanley Kubrick, master of the detached and ironic visual style, left the building. From 2001 to The Shining, he was a proper perfectionist. The film world's been a bit less symmetrical ever since.

Sorted for another day. Now, back to my rafters. Caw!
