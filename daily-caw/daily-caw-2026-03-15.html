March 15, 1940

The Silent Steal: Nazi Seizure of Church Bells

Blimey, imagine waking up and finding the local bell tower's gone quiet because some tyrant needs more bronze for his armaments. On this day in 1940, Hitler's second-in-command, Hermann Göring, issued the "Reclaiming all Bells for War Purposes" decree. They didn't just take German bells; they plundered Austria, Belgium, France, Poland, and even the Vatican.

About 175,000 bells were snatched, and 150,000 of 'em were melted down. Bronze is four parts copper and one part tin, both proper useful for making guns and gear. The locals didn't take it lying down, though. Some buried their bells deep, and one defiant Belgian priest barred his doors and Rang 'em loud while the Nazis were at the gate. Proper legend.

Crow's Eyewitness Note: After the war, thousands of bells were found in "bell cemeteries," which actually helped experts learn more about campanology—the art of bell ringing. Every cloud, eh?

Beware the Ides of March: Caesar's Final Curtain

A classic bit of political drama. Julius Caesar, the man who thought he was untouchable, met his end at the hands of his own senators. Brutus and the gang decided he'd grown a bit too big for his boots. It's the original "taking the piss" turned deadly. History's most famous backstabbing, literally.
