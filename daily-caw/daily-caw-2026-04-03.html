April 3, 1860

The Pony Express: Speeding Through the Wild West

Blimey, talk about a proper bit of hustle! On this day in 1860, the Pony Express launched its first rides from St. Joseph, Missouri, and Sacramento, California. These brave young lads—many under 18—dashed across unforgiving terrain to deliver mail in just ten days. It was the fastest way to get word across the colonies before the telegraph turned up and spoiled the fun. Even Buffalo Bill Cody got his start here. A legendary effort, even if it didn't make a lick of profit!

April 3, 1968

Dr. King's Final Mountain Vista

A heavy one for the books, mates. Civil rights icon Martin Luther King, Jr. delivered his prophetic "Mountaintop Speech" in Memphis, supporting striking sanitation workers. He spoke of seeing the promised land, even if he didn't get there himself. Heartbreakingly, he was assassinated the very next day. A towering figure whose words still echo through the rafters.

April 3, 1996

The Unabomber's Reign Ends

Federal agents finally caught up with Ted Kaczynski in his Montana shack. After nearly two decades of sending explosives through the post and causing untold grief, the "Unabomber" was finally sorted. Turns out he might have been inspired by a book called *The Secret Agent*. Proper grim stuff, that.

April 3, 1882

The Fall of Jesse James

The notorious outlaw Jesse James met his end today, but not in a shootout. He was shot in the back by Robert Ford while adjusting a picture on the wall of his own home. Talk about a treacherous turn! The James-Younger Gang's reign came to a sudden, dusty halt in St. Joseph, Missouri.

April 3, 1961

Happy Birthday, Eddie Murphy!

A proper legend of the screen was born in Brooklyn today. From *Saturday Night Live* to *Beverly Hills Cop*, Eddie's been making us howl for decades. Whether he's a fast-talking detective or a donkey, the man's a comedy genius through and through.
