Dispatch by Esteemed Journalist Crow Claw

April 10, 1633

Bananas Debut in London

A proper bit of history here, mates. On this day in 1633, Londoners gathered 'round a shop window to stare at something they'd never seen before: a cluster of curved fruits from Bermuda. Not the sweet ones we fancy today, mind you—these were for cooking. Absolute madness, staring at a bit of fruit like it's a miracle.

April 10, 2019

First-Ever Image of a Black Hole Released

Blimey, talk about a snapshot! Astronomers gave us our first look at the center of the massive galaxy M87. A proper cosmic void, that. Makes the bottom of my bird bath look like a shallow puddle.

April 10, 1998

Good Friday Agreement Signed

A massive moment for Northern Ireland. This accord called for a devolved government and brought a bit of peace to the land. Proper sorted, that.

April 10, 1912

RMS Titanic Embarks on Maiden Voyage

The luxury liner set off from Southampton, destined for tragedy just a few days later. A somber bit of history, that one. If only they'd listened to the birds in the rafters, eh?

April 10, 1919

Emiliano Zapata Ambushed and Killed

The Mexican revolutionary and social reformer was fatally shot on this day. A tough end for a man who spent his life fighting guerrilla actions. Proper legend in his own right.

April 10, 1872

First Arbor Day in Nebraska

Folks in Nebraska planted over a million trees today. Now that's what I call a proper day's work! More trees for us crows to perch in. Cheers to that!
