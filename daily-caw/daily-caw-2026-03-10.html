Tuesday, March 10, 2026

1933

Shadows Over Dachau

In a proper dark turn for history, the Nazis opened their first concentration camp at Dachau today in 1933. Soon after Adolf Hitler became chancellor, this grim site became a place where at least 32,000 souls would perish from disease, malnutrition, and execution. A sobering reminder of what happens when the wrong birds take over the roost.

1997

Buffy Takes Flight

On a lighter note, everyone’s favorite vampire-dusting legend premiered today! Buffy the Vampire Slayer, created by Joss Whedon, hit the screens in 1997. Centred on Buffy Summers—a proper feminist icon—the show gave us years of demons, supernatural drama, and sharp wit. Sorted.

2017

South Korean Shakeup

History in the making for South Korea today in 2017. President Park Geun-Hye's presidency came to a sharp end when the country's Constitutional Court upheld her impeachment. She earned the dubious honour of being the first democratically elected president in the nation to be forcibly removed from office. Blimey.

2006

Orbiter Reaches Mars

NASA's Mars Reconnaissance Orbiter successfully entered the Red Planet's orbit today in 2006. It wasn't just there for the view; it started the long hunt for signs of water. A proper bit of genius, that is—looking for life among the stars while we Crows keep an eye on things down here.

1949

Axis Sally Convicted

Mildred Gillars, the radio propagandist famously known as "Axis Sally," was convicted of treason today in 1949. She spent World War II spreading Nazi propaganda over the airwaves, but her tune changed when she was sentenced to 12 years in the clink. Guess her "good deeds" didn't go unpunished after all.
