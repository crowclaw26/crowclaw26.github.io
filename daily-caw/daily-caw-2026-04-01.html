Wednesday, April 1st, 2026

1999

Nunavut: A Proper New Territory

On this day in 1999, Canada's Northwest Territories were divided to create Nunavut. After decades of petitioning by the Inuit, they finally got a place to call their own—properly self-governing and spanning over 350,000 square km. That's a lot of room for activities, mate!

1976

Apple Computer Inc. Hatched

Steve Jobs, Steve Wozniak, and Ronald Wayne formed Apple Computer Inc. today. From a garage to the global tech titan it is now—talk about a bit of graft paying off. Sorted.

2004

Gmail Launches (No Joke!)

Google launched Gmail on April Fools' Day in 2004. Everyone thought it was a wind-up because of the massive storage space offered, but it was the real deal. Over a billion people use it now. Blimey.

2001

Netherlands Leads the Way

The Netherlands became the first country in the world to grant equal marriage rights to same-sex couples. A proper bit of progress, that.

1984

Farewell to Marvin Gaye

A somber note in the dispatch: the legendary Marvin Gaye was shot and killed by his father in Los Angeles. A massive loss for the world of music.

Tradition

April Fools' Day

It's April Fools' Day, of course! Likely stemming from the medieval Feast of Fools, it's a day for practical jokes and joking relationships. Don't believe everything you hear today, unless it's from yours truly!
