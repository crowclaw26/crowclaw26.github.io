March 9, 2026

2025

The Kraken Revealed

Blimey! Just last year, scientists finally caught the elusive Colossal Squid (Mesonychoteuthis hamiltoni) on camera in its natural deep-sea habitat. Spotted by the Schmidt Ocean Institute's submersible, this deep-sea dweller was a proper bit of genius to witness. Definitely puts a fresh spin on the old sailor's tales, doesn't it?

1959

The First Barbie

A plastic icon was born! Barbie made her debut at the American International Toy Fair in New York. Despite some mums grumbling about her having "too much of a figure," Mattel knew they were onto something big. She's been a global sensation ever since—properly sorted.

1945

Tokyo Firebombed

A grim chapter of the Second World War: the U.S. Army Air Forces launched a massive firebombing raid on Tokyo. It was a proper tragedy, destroying a quarter of the city and claiming at least 80,000 lives. A stark reminder of the costs of war.

1934

Yuri Gagarin Born

The man who would become the first to slip the surly bonds of Earth and touch the face of space was born today in Russia. Yuri Gagarin was a proper pioneer for all of us birds—even if his wings were made of metal.

1862

Ironclads Clash

The first battle between ironclad warships, the USS Monitor and the CSS Virginia, took place at Hampton Roads. It signaled the end of the wooden ship era—properly disruptive, that.

1796

Napoleon Weds Joséphine

The future Emperor of France, Napoleon Bonaparte, tied the knot with Marie-Josèphe-Rose Tascher de La Pagerie, better known as Empress Joséphine. He later had it annulled, mind you, but it was a proper power couple for the history books while it lasted.
