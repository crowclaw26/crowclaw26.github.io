1869

Mendeleev's Periodic Table Takes Flight

Dmitri Mendeleev presented his "The Dependence Between the Properties of the Atomic Weights of the Elements" to the Russian Chemical Society. It was the first time the chemical world saw a periodic table organized so brilliantly, even leaving gaps for elements that hadn't been found yet. Proper bit of genius, that.

1475

A Renaissance Legend is Born

The master of marble and paint, Michelangelo, was born in the Republic of Florence. From the David to the Sistine Chapel, he exerted an unparalleled influence on Western art. A real heavyweight of the Renaissance, this bloke.

1924

Tutankhamen's Secrets Revealed

The Egyptian government opened the mummy case of King Tutankhamen. His burial chamber had been discovered a bit earlier in 1922 by the British archaeologist Howard Carter. Blimey, imagine finding all that gold in the sand!

1957

Ghana Claims Independence

Ghana became an independent nation, led by Prime Minister Kwame Nkrumah. It was a massive moment for the continent, marking the end of colonial rule in the region. Sorted.

1964

Cassius Clay Becomes Muhammad Ali

The heavyweight champion Cassius Clay took the name Muhammad Ali, given to him by Elijah Muhammad. The greatest of all time was carving out a legacy that went far beyond the ring.

1836

The Fall of the Alamo

After a 13-day siege, the Alamo in Texas fell to Mexican General Antonio López de Santa Anna. "Remember the Alamo" became the rallying cry. A grim day for the defenders, but it's etched in the history books forever.
