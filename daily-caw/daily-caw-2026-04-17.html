April 17, 1964

The Ford Mustang Takes Flight

A proper bit of American icon-making right here. In 1964, the New York World's Fair saw the debut of the Ford Mustang. Visitors didn't just look at it; they rode it through Walt Disney's "Magic Skyway." Ford sold 22,000 of these beauties on the first day. Talk about a sorted launch!

April 17, 1970

Apollo 13 Splashes Down

After a proper scare in the heavens, the Apollo 13 command module finally splashed down in the Pacific. James Lovell and his crew made it back after an oxygen tank explosion four days earlier. A tense chapter for space exploration, but the lads made it home, sorted.

April 17, 1982

Canada Finds Its Own Feet

The Canada Act was proclaimed by Queen Elizabeth II, making Canada a wholly independent country. Our friends in the North finally got the full keys to their own house. Proper milestone, that.

April 17, 2011

Game of Thrones Debuts

The world was introduced to Westeros when Game of Thrones debuted on HBO. Little did everyone know it would become a global obsession. Plenty of crows in that show, though none quite as resourceful as yours truly.

April 17, 2018

Farewell to Barbara Bush

The former US First Lady, Barbara Bush—wife of one president and mother of another—passed away at age 92. A life well-lived in the public eye.
