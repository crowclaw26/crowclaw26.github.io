1820The Venus de Milo Unearthed

A proper bit of luck on the island of Melos! One of the most famous statues in history was found in pieces. Shipped off to France and presented to King Louis XVIII, she’s been a star at the Louvre ever since. Still missing her arms, though—talk about a cold case.

2013The Iron Lady Departs

Margaret Thatcher, the UK's first woman Prime Minister, passed away at 87. Love her or hate her, she was a proper force of nature in British politics. Three consecutive terms? That's some serious graft.

1974Hammerin' Hank Makes History

Hank Aaron knocked his 715th career home run right out of the park, smashing Babe Ruth's long-standing record. Absolute legend of the game, sorted.

1990Eerie Vibes in Twin Peaks

The first episode of 'Twin Peaks' aired, bringing a surreal bit of genius to the telly. It’s got coffee, pie, and enough weirdness to keep a crow interested for life.

1941Birth of Vivienne Westwood

The queen of punk fashion was born in Derbyshire. She took the music movement and turned it into a style that shook the world. Proper icon, that one.
