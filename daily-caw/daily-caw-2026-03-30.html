March 30, 1867

Alaska: A Frosty Bargain

U.S. Secretary of State William H. Seward struck a deal to purchase Alaska from Russia for $7.2 million. Mocked at the time as "Seward's Folly," the critics piped down once the Klondike gold rush kicked off. Proper bargain, that.

March 30, 1853

The Birth of a Master

Vincent van Gogh, the Dutch Post-Impressionist legend, was born in Groot Zundert. A man of immense talent and deep anguish, he'd go on to change the art world forever. Proper genius, even if he was a bit troubled.

March 30, 2002

Farewell to the Queen Mother

Elizabeth, the Queen Mother, passed away in her sleep at Windsor Castle at the ripe old age of 101. She was the queen consort of the United Kingdom and a proper icon of the 20th century.

March 30, 1981

A Close Call for Reagan

Barely two months into his presidency, Ronald Reagan was shot and wounded by John W. Hinckley, Jr. in Washington, D.C. A shock to the system, but the man pulled through.

March 30, 1945

Slowhand Arrives

British rock legend Eric Clapton was born. From the Yardbirds to Cream and a massive solo career, he's a proper guitar god. Sorted.
